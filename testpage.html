Hello world! You found me!

Wait, who did you find exactly?
My name is Karina and I'm a software engineer living in San Francisco, CA. I currently work as a software, mostly front-end web, engineer on the Microsoft Office Analytics team. I spend most of my time designing and coding up an analytics platform to help make Microsoft more data driven.

I have a passion for beautiful things, whether it'd be beautiful code, user experiences, graphic design, or data visualizations. Thus, my mind usually tends to focus mainly on anything that is user-facing. I take pride in being a mix of many things.

So, what am I?

I'm an adventurer.

As a recent graduate, my primary purpose is to experience as many things as I can to find my true niche in the world. Culture is very important to me, and therefore, so is traveling and seeing the world. I believe it is impossible to truly empathize with those cut from cloths other than our own, but I believe that to be a truly global community we must go out and learn as much as we can from each other.

I'm a learner.

Curiosity may have killed the cat, but at least it was brave to reach beyond comfort and mediocrity. Similar to adventuring, I think it's important to experience new things. Familiar situations make me uncomfortable and anxious, to the point at which I feel I am wasting my time not continuing to learn. So, I'll almost always be willing to try a new game, hobby, or food. Just ask me!

I'm a creative.

A wonderful professor of mine at Carnegie Mellon (Dan Licata) once concluded one of his lectures with a single slide stating "Code is art", which is exactly how I have since treated my software engineering/front-end development career. Any creative career requires intuition and unique problem solving abilities, and I'd like to think I have a creative's intuition.
